# Lakatos - "Proofs and Refutations" (1976)

## Core Thesis

Mathematics grows NOT through steady accumulation of truths, but through a **dialectical process** of conjectures, attempted proofs, refutations, and concept refinement.

## The Method of Proofs and Refutations

### Stages (from Appendix I):

1. **Primitive conjecture** - initial guess
2. **Proof** - rough thought-experiment decomposing the conjecture into subconjectures
3. **Global counterexamples** - emerge, refuting the primitive conjecture
4. **Proof re-examined** - the "guilty lemma" is spotted, made explicit, built into the conjecture as a condition. The improved conjecture supersedes the primitive.

### Further stages:
5. Other theorems are examined for the new lemma/concept
6. Accepted consequences of the refuted conjecture are checked
7. Counterexamples are turned into new examples - new fields of inquiry open up

## Key Concepts

### Monster-barring
When a counterexample violates an implicit assumption, redefine the objects to exclude it.

### Exception-barring
Declare the counterexample an "exception" outside the domain.

### Method of surrender
Abandon the conjecture.

### Lemma-incorporation
Absorb the guilty lemma as a hypothesis.

## The Heuristic vs. Deductivist Approach

| Deductivist | Heuristic |
|-------------|-----------|
| Definition -> Theorem -> Proof | Problem -> Conjecture -> Proof/Refutation |
| Hides the process | Shows the process |
| Static | Dynamic |
| Mature mathematics | Mathematics in the making |

## Key Quotes

- "Mathematics grows instead through a richer, more dramatic process of the successive improvement of creative hypotheses by attempts to 'prove' them and by criticism of these attempts."
- "Logic sometimes makes monsters." (quoting Hadamard)
- "The deductivist style hides the struggle."

## Implications for IntuitMath

- **This is THE foundational text for our project** - the dialectical method IS the mathematician's thinking process
- We should explicitly structure responses as: Conjecture -> Attempt -> Refinement -> New Conjecture
- The "monster-barring" and "lemma-incorporation" techniques should be named in our skill
- The heuristic style should be our default; deductivist style is the "strict version"
- The idea that definitions are not carved in stone - they evolve through counterexamples
